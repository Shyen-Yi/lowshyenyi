    Industrial Design

    Zero Waste

    Zero Waste is a series of products that strive to improve the current shopping habits of customers. There is a lot of food waste and energy consumption from the consumer products food chain to the of the product that lands to the customer’s hands. The goal is to design a complete system to integrate the communities, wholesale food suppliers, and framers to minimize waste. The concept of using recyclable bags and implement the virtual shopping experience that improves the customer shopping experience. The recyclable product will be produced by the community center that will allocate the investment back to the locals. The entire system is about to create a loop to enhance better life quality and connection to the whole of the food system.

    Industrial Design

    Joi

    Joi is a new solution for bike saddle design. It aims to youngsters or whoever doesn’t want to sacrifice a comfortable riding experience but wants to carry another person on your bike. The butterfly shape allowed the bike to take another person in the back comfortably. It is designed for the short and mid-range ride from point A to B. Joi is also design for ease releasing from the bike pose as well as ease carrying for the user for security purposes. The user can easily strap the saddle on the bag or simply hold it by hand. If you are the person who would love to hand out with your friend and love riding your bike in the city, JOI is the design for you.

    Public Art Installation

    MobilEyez

    MobilEyez eyes are an art grant project from Basscoast Music Festival. I am honour to be one of the members at Orb Weaver Collective in collaborating on this project. MobilEyez is a hanging mobile designed to entrain audiences into a state of wonder and curiosity. Each piece provides the counterweight for the piece above, creating a mind-bending dynamic system of opposing forces suspended in complete balance. The mobile morphs in response to atmospheric flows of air, creating forms that ride the line between chaos and order. A cozy net invites people to focus upward towards the ephemeral faces of the sculpture and sky. Safe and suspended MobilEyes provides a space to reflect and to reconcile the opposing forces of the cosmos.

    Public art Installation

    Geodesic Dome

    Geodesic Dome is a reuse project from one of the Cabin Fever display in the Vancouver Art Gallery. It was initially designed to be shown on the ground, we came out with an idea of why not suspended to the ceiling and install a light in the middle. So people can have a different experience perspective when looking at the dome. It provides two very contrasting atmospheric feelings in the separation of the space. We like the strong contrasting of it to let people have the instant feedback of the art installation.
